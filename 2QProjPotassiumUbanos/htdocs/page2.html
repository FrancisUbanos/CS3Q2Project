Nintendo Switch Screen

The Nintendo Switch in its base form has 2 parts, the nintendo switch itself, and its joy cons. The Nintendo Switch or others may call it the screen is where things like the power button, volume button, and even speakers are located, with 2 rails on its sides, this is where the joy cons are attached and a port at the bottom which is for the dock.


































Joy Cons

Joy cons are the base controllers of the nintendo switch which upon purchase you get two. Their size, though it may seem small, has a lot of features to it, with a start button, joystick, 4 buttons, l and r buttons on the top, etc. Thats why if you have any many games that usually need an controller like a pro controller, a joy con can replace it.


































Extras/Others

Now that we have stated the base parts, the “extra” parts that add to the experience (these items come with the nintendo switch), as said previously the nintendo switch can be docked, which means that you can connect it to a device like a television through an html cord. Joy Con straps allow you to strap the joy con into your arm so that it won't fall off if you let go, this is useful in games like Just Dance. The rest of the parts are for setting up the charger, joy con grip (which will be discussed later on), and an AC adapter.
