Joy Con Grip

There are many controllers that the switch can support, but here I will talk about the 3 base controllers, starting off with the grip joy cons, the grip is a port or device that can turn the two joy cons attached into a controller you can use both hands on with more space, this allows for more buttons available (compared to a single joy con), it does come with the package when buying a switch buy you can of course buy more online if you have more than a pair of joycons.


































Pro Controller

The pro controller is a wireless controller that utilizes a mix of button layouts, more specifically from that of a GameCuba controller, Wii's Classic Controller Pro, and an Xbox Wireless Controller. With this design a player has the options of 1 movement and analog stick, a dpad, 2 shoulder buttons, etc. One thing to note is that this does not come with the Nintendo Switch upon purchasing. With being wireless it is bluetooth, this indicates that you can connect this controller to other devices like your laptop.


































GameCube Controller

Based on the original GameCube controller, this gives the players a wireless version of that, although this may not be as beginner friendly as the pro controller, it does offer the option for players who are used to this buttom layout, this type of controller is mainly used in fighting games like Super Smash Brothers Ultimate, but can also be used for other games. Like the Pro controller this has to be purchased separately and can be connected to other devices due to bluetooth.
